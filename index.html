Three Decades of Innovation

The ThinkPad is a line of business-oriented laptops that were originally designed, manufactured, and sold by IBM starting in 1992. In 2005, Lenovo acquired IBM's personal computer division, thereby acquiring the renowned ThinkPad brand and perpetuating its legacy of engineering excellence.

The distinctive industrial design of the ThinkPad was developed in 1992 by Richard Sapper, who drew inspiration from the clean, compartmentalized form of a traditional Japanese bento lunchbox. That design philosophy — functional, unadorned, and purposeful — continues to define the line to this day.

ThinkPads have a proven track record of reliability, having been used for years as the standard laptop on the International Space Station. From the office to the field, the ThinkPad is a reliable tool for those who demand dependability above all else.
